# Citation chaining procedure

## Definition

In citation chaining, one journal article <mark style="color:red;">links you to another</mark>, which <mark style="color:red;">links you to another</mark>, and so on to create a chain of relevant literature.

This is a useful research tactic when you are working on a literature review since it helps you follow chains of related sources.

For example, let's say you've found a relevant journal article on your topic. You can make a chain of citations leading from that one article both forwards and backwards from the year of publication.

## Procedure

Let’s say we’re reading Miles Hwestone’s article, [Intergroup Bias](https://drive.google.com/open?id=1jGMCJI9NAs82EjxY-Z9xKV8VGvNh6umx\&usp=drive_fs).&#x20;

## How to find articles

We will find the full PDF versions of these three articles.&#x20;

We will paste the DOI (most preferably) or title of the articles into [scholar.google.com](http://scholar.google.com).

If we see <mark style="color:red;">\[PDF] on the right side</mark>, we have access. We click on \[PDF], **not** the title.

## How to find DOI?

It's always better to search DOI instead of title of the articles.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (99).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

You can also use [crossref.org/guestquery](https://www.crossref.org/guestquery/)

* Paste the "First Author" name and the "Article Title"
* Click Search
* The DOI appears at the bottom
